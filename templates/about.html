About this data set

The original data set was found on kaggle at www.kaggle.com/currie32/crimes-in-chicago. The data includes information on all crimes committed and recorded by the City of Chicago from 2001 to the present date minus the most recent seven days. The data includes detailed information about each incidient including case number, various levels of classification of the crime, locations (ranging from coordinates to region of city), and information on when the crime was committed.

Where did this data come from?

Is the data complete?

If not, what is missing and what concerns does it raise? What is your evidence for this?

This data is not complete becuase there is a lack of data for 2014-2016. While doing our analysis of crime by season we found that there was no information for Summer and Fall in 2016 and a large amoung of missing data for Fall 2014 as well and Summer and Fall 2015. The graph below that is aggregated by season per year visibly shows the missing data in our set. This lack of data causes the overall number of crimes to appear to decrease from 2012-2016 drastically, but further investigation showed that there was simply a lack of data in the set for these times.


Is the data coherent?

Does it make sense relative to itself? Does it match your expectations? Are the distributions sensible? What is your evidence for this?

The data on the locations of where most crimes occur and where the most violent crimes occur does make sense. The southern wards and community areas of chicago have more crime and higher rates of violent crime and this makes sense becuase this is what is traditionally reported in the media and these areas have higher poverty and minority rates.

The violent versus non-violent data and ratios also do make sense because we expected that a majority of crimes committed would be petty crime like theft and not actually violent

The one part of our data that does not make sense is the overall number of crimes per year. The data we have shows a drastic descrease from 2012 to 2016 and this does not makes sense. However,we did discover that this drop is most likely due to incomplete data for 2014, 2015, and 2016.

Is the data correct?

What aspects of this data can you verify? How did you do so? What did you find?

The data from Kaggle was not correct because of the data that was missing. We cross referenced data available from the Data Portal of the City of Chicago and found different numbers for the total count of crimes that were more consistent across all four years and higher than our data. The City of Chicago Data Portal data can be found here. With this in mind the records themselves that were present in the Kaggle data were correct and accurate.
