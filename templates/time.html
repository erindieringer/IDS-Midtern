Time Series Data

With our maps we were able to get a wholistic view of where crimes happen in Chicago in general and based on certain events. However, we also thought it would be useful to look at the trends for when crimes happen throughout the year. We wanted to test the common theory that there are more crimes when the weather is warmer by looking at the number of crimes by season from 2012-2016.

Looking at the data holistically, we wanted to get a snapshot of the daily crimes being committed by year. You can interact with the graph below to see certain spikes or drops in the data.

In the data, each crime was categorized into 32 main categories. Because of all the publicity surrounding homicide and murder rates in Chicago, we wanted to explore what were actually the most popular crimes being committed. The graph below shows a snapshot of what the most common crimes were by number of crimes committed from 2012 to 2016.



We further grouped the various crime times into violent and non-violent crime based on the FBI classifications (details here). The graph below shows the percentage of violent versus non-violent crime by season for 2012-2016. The graph shows what we predicted and what the previous two graphs show: a majority of crimes committed in Chicago are not classified as violent crime.
